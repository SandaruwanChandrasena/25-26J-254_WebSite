Project Scope

Research Foundation of ScreenMind

ScreenMind addresses the growing mental health impact of excessive smartphone use through an integrated AI-based detection and early intervention platform.

Introduction

Smartphones and social media are deeply embedded in daily life, but excessive usage is strongly linked to stress, anxiety, depression, poor sleep, and reduced focus. Existing tools often provide only screen-time tracking without meaningful mental health insights.

Research Problem

The project investigates how smartphone behavior, sleep-related data, communication signals, and screen usage patterns can be analyzed with AI to detect early mental health risks and support timely preventive action.

Research Gap

Current systems mainly focus on usage statistics, self-reporting, or isolated data sources. They lack holistic, real-time, privacy-aware, and explainable solutions for smartphone-addiction-related well-being issues.

Research Objectives

  • Predict mental health risks from screen usage logs
  • Analyze social media interaction patterns for distress signals
  • Detect social isolation and loneliness trends
  • Estimate sleep disruption risks using phone-based sensor data

Methodology

The methodology combines passive smartphone sensing, local feature extraction, AI/ML-based analysis, risk scoring, explainable results, and actionable interventions through a mobile-first architecture.

Technologies Used

React Native Kotlin Python FastAPI Firebase SQLite LSTM BiLSTM RoBERTa TensorFlow

System Overview

How the ScreenMind Platform Works

ScreenMind functions as a digital phenotyping ecosystem that collects privacy-aware behavioral signals, processes meaningful features, applies AI models, and presents interpretable mental health risk insights to users.

Data Collection

Smartphone usage, notifications, mobility indicators, proximity signals, and sleep-related behavior are captured with privacy-aware methods.

AI Processing

Extracted behavioral features are analyzed through weighted logic, LSTM/BiLSTM models, and transformer-based sentiment analysis.

Risk Insights

Users receive low, moderate, or high risk insights, explainable summaries, and proactive recommendations to improve well-being.

Key Features

Four Intelligent Detection Modules

ScreenMind is built around four interconnected modules that monitor digital behavior and identify different dimensions of mental health risk.

Mental Health Predictor from Screen Usage Logs

Combines real screen usage data with validated mental health questionnaires to estimate user risk levels and provide actionable dashboard insights.

Social Media Interaction Pattern Analysis

Uses AI-driven sentiment analysis on social media notification patterns to detect negative emotional exposure and generate real-time warning insights.

Social Isolation & Loneliness Risk Detection

Monitors mobility, communication, behavioral, and proximity patterns to identify early signs of social withdrawal and loneliness through explainable AI risk analysis.

Sleep Disruption Risk Estimation

Tracks late-night phone behavior and phone-based sensor activity to estimate sleep disruption risk and deliver personalized preventive guidance.
